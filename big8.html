The Big 8 App

Problem

There are no apps that locate restaurants with a zero tolerance policy toward certain ingredient allergens (ingredient is not allowed in the restaurant at any time for any reason)

Questions to figure out:

• What is needed?

• Who is it for?

• Where will it live?

• Can it be designed so that the main functionality of the app and user activity is contained within one or two screens?

In a nutshell

smartphone app interface for finding a specific food within a given area

My take

app lets you search for restaurants that don't utilize specific food allergens

Why

people with high sensitivities to certain allergens need an app to show them where they can eat

Who

solo project

Technologies

Sketch

Food allergies affect millions of people around the world. Are you tired of going to a restaurant, only to find out you can’t have anything on the menu? Even if the food you order doesn’t directly utilize ingredients you’re allergic to, food cross-contamination is still a risk for people with high-sensitivity allergies.

This smartphone app lets you search for restaurants that have a zero tolerance for any one or combination of the 8 main allergens in their restaurant, meaning their use is not allowed in the entire restaurant. The Big 8 refers to the most common food allergies: eggs, fish, milk, peanuts, shellfish, soy, tree nuts, and wheat.

This app is for people with food allergies or anyone who knows someone with food allergies and wants to protect them. It allows for peace of mind as you don’t need to fear that the food is contaminated with an allergen. Keep in mind, this app recognizes the most common “Big 8” food allergens and does not cover all potential food allergens.

First, choose which allergen or allergens to avoid. Write in a city or zip code to search an area for restaurants. The app is not limited to local searches because it’s important to be able to search far away when planning a trip. Next, move your slider to select your maximum search radius. Finally, click search and a map displaying viable restaurants will display.

Mood Board

For the main colors, I chose those only found in nature. Peach, greens, and purples are all colors found in foods, appropriate for a food app.

Pattern Library

I wanted to make this app approachable for people of any age, as food allergens don’t discriminate! The icons are simple, yet convey the food they are referring to.

Mockup

Toggle elements, while perhaps not quite as prevalent in design as they used to be, here create strong clarity for your selection. Further helping the user, your selections are displayed on the next screen, where the restaurant results are displayed on a map.
