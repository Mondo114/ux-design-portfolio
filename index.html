Food Delivery Website Project Brief:

Cal Arts Assignment: "Imagine that you've been hired by an entrepreneur who wants to open a new, delivery-only establishment that delivers food (or drinks) to customers living nearby. You have been asked to plan and design the restaurant’s website. The centerpiece of this website will be an online food customization and ordering system."

Project Ideas

Diabetic Delights - Aimed at senior citizens - San Luis Obispo - $$ - Meals targeted to diabetic senior citizens so they don’t have to worry about what they can and can’t have - Problem: Not super familiar with diabetic needs, would need to learn more Microgurt - Aimed at families - Los Angeles South Bay - $$ - Subscription-based, made-to-order yogurt deliver that uses high quality ingredients - Problem: Ensuring live cultures stay active
Custom Creamery - Aimed at businesses - Downtown Los Angeles - $$$ - Made to order ice cream with customizable high-end ingredients - Problem: No real problems; I just liked the yogurt idea more Tanoshī Ramen - Aimed at college students - Westwood, Los Angeles - $ - Made to order ramen with customizable ingredients - Problem: Keeping ramen in the best condition for transportation can be difficult; not as creative

Research

Restaurant Name & URL Microgurt www.microgurt.com (not currently in actual use) Elevator pitch Microgurt is a subscription-based, made-to-order yogurt delivery company that uses high quality ingredients with a focus on providing a substantial quantity of probiotic microorganisms to establish and maintain a happy digestive system. Location Los Angeles South Bay Main target audience - Families - New parents Cost - Mid-level price: made in small batches with high-quality ingredients - About $6.00 for 1 qt - 1 qt is minimum purchase - Suggested subscription: 1 qt per person per week (about ½ cup eaten each day)
Food & drink Subscription-based, custom probiotic yogurt for delivery Base: Cream + Milk (regular) Skim Milk (low fat) Coconut Cream + Coconut Milk (non-dairy) + Add-Ins: Fruits Nuts Nut Butters Spices Honey Agave Nectar Molasses Vanilla Malt Powder Cocoa Powder + Probiotics (can make several add-in categories like “sweeteners”)

Strategy

Target Audience

Roles Parents who play sports and/or have children in sports Parents or their children who have gastrointestinal maladies Parents who do yoga and/or have health-oriented goals for their family
Demographics:
Age: Parents: 25 - 45 Children: 2 - 17
Gender: More women, but some men also Education: Mostly college educated Marital Status: Mostly married Income: Middle-to-Upper Location: Urban to suburban

User Personas

Kristen Roberts
Age: 36 Profession: Part-time yoga instructor Spouse: Bill (35) Kids: 1 daughter, Emma (4) Values: Health-focused In free time: When not instructing yoga, she is at home making soaps, candles, and many other natural products to ship through sales from her website Issues: Between working and husband being gone a lot, she doesn’t have much time for cooking and has to order a lot of the family’s groceries online Possible Solution: Add healthy yogurt (protein, vitamins, probiotics) to her family’s diet and be able to get it through online orders
David Valdes
Age: 42 Profession: Construction Superintendent Spouse: Maria (40) Kids: 2 sons, Michael (6), William (3) Values: Family-oriented In free time: Spends lots of time at the beach with his family; does nearly everything outside of work with his family Issues: David has gastrointestinal discomfort Possible Solution: When taken daily, probiotics in Microgurt may help in alleviating David’s gastrointestinal discomfort

User Needs

Website needs to enable the user to: - Find out if the supplier delivers to their area - Order product online - Product description and custom ingredient ordering - Set up subscription orders and scheduling - Estimate prices - See order history - Contact someone with questions or issues about an order

Client Needs

Website needs to enable the user to: - To sell product online that will be delivered - Provide a system for order customization - Reliable communication and customer support - Communicate quality of the ingredients - Communicate health benefits of probiotics

Outline of Scope

Content Requirements

Food Menu - show a list of pre-planned yogurt product as well as ingredients to choose from for custom yogurts - display pricing Images of product Text indicating product is for South Bay area only Contact section - list company phone number and email address; it may be necessary to have multiple numbers and emails for: - ordering - customer inquiries / support About section - describe the company and the quality of the ingredients Educational section - explain probiotics and best methods of consuming Testimonials FAQ section - where do you deliver? - what about dietary restrictions (allergens, dairy, etc.) - minimum order? - additional charge for delivery? - minimum time need for an order? - pricing structure? - contact?

Functionality Requirements

User enters delivery address and finds out if she is close enough to receive deliveries Form to fill out and submit for orders; add items to shopping cart Form to fill out for making changes / cancellation if necessary Purchase as a guest or create a user account Log in In addition to accessing order history, logging in also allows user to set up a subscription for regular intervals of delivery (probiotics are best consumed daily for beneficial results) - time and date of delivery Place order - submit order form - enter payment information - get order confirmation (on website and receive email)
