Microgurt Food Delivery Website

Course assignment

"Imagine that you've been hired by an entrepreneur who wants to open a new, delivery-only establishment that delivers food (or drinks) to customers living nearby. You have been asked to plan and design the restaurant’s website. The centerpiece of this website will be an online food customization and ordering system."

In a nutshell

website interface for food delivery company

My take

made-to-order yogurt delivery company

Why

assignment for the California Institute of the Arts course

Who

solo project

Technologies

Sketch, Balsamiq, InVision, Paint.NET

This project showcases my process from start to finish. It goes through preliminary research stages and sets up a strategy. The outline of scope shows what I will need to include for content and functionality. The sitemap sets up the content hierarchy.

Next, wireframes introduce the content and where it will go on the page. Mockups add color, typography, and design detail. The interactive high-fidelity prototype allows the user to test out the website without all the requirements of building the actual website.

Wireframes These interactive wireframes show the website navigation menu options changing depending on which page the user is on. I later changed this as can be seen in the high-fidelity prototypes. I found it made more sense to keep the menu choices always the same for consistency, and simply alter the color of the name of the page you’re currently on. Individual wireframes are displayed lower on this page. Wireframes were created in Balsamiq.

Prototype This interactive prototype displays the ordering and checkout processes for customers. While checking out, each page displays relevant content on the left and breadcrumbs on the right to show the customer where they are in the process. The About page is also available for view to give additional flavor of other pages of the site. Individual prototype pages are displayed lower on this page. Prototype was created in Sketch + InVision.

Microgurt is a subscription-based, made-to-order yogurt delivery company that uses high quality ingredients with a focus on providing a substantial quantity of probiotic microorganisms to establish and maintain a happy digestive system.

User Needs

Website needs to enable the user to:

- Find out if the supplier delivers to their area

- Order product online

- Discern product description and custom ingredient ordering

- Set up subscription orders and scheduling

- Estimate prices

- See order history

- Contact someone with questions or issues about an order

Client Needs

Website needs to enable the client to:

- Sell product online that will be delivered

- Provide a system for order customization

- Provide reliable communication and customer support

- Communicate quality of the ingredients

- Communicate health benefits of probiotics

Mood Board

The dark purples were used for buttons, but other than that, most colors were much lighter. As the website is selling a yogurt product, I wanted to go with soft colors that almost look like they could be considered “creamy”. The foods here depict high-quality ingredients. When ordering food, it’s important to have photos of the food you’re ordering. An addition I might make to this website if it were launched would be to have info pages of each ingredient. These pages could have information like country of origin and a photo. Mood Board was created in Sketch.
