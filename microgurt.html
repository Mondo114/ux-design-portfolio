Microgurt Food Delivery Website Project

Course assignment: "Imagine that you've been hired by an entrepreneur who wants to open a new, delivery-only establishment that delivers food (or drinks) to customers living nearby. You have been asked to plan and design the restaurant’s website. The centerpiece of this website will be an online food customization and ordering system."

In a nutshell: website interface for food delivery company

My take: made-to-order yogurt delivery company

Why: assignment for the California Institute of the Arts course

Who: solo project

Technologies: Sketch, Balsamiq, InVision, Paint.NET (similar to Photoshop)

This project showcases my process from start to finish. It goes though preliminary research stages and sets up a strategy. The outline of scope shows what I will need to include, content and functionality-wise. The sitemap sets up the content hierarchy.

Next, wireframes introduce the content and where it will go on the page. Mockups add color, typography, and design detail. The interactive high-fidelity prototype allows the user to test out the website without all the requirements of building the actual website.

Research

Restaurant Name & URL Microgurt www.microgurt.com (not currently in actual use) Elevator pitch Microgurt is a subscription-based, made-to-order yogurt delivery company that uses high quality ingredients with a focus on providing a substantial quantity of probiotic microorganisms to establish and maintain a happy digestive system. Location Los Angeles South Bay Main target audience - Families - New parents Cost - Mid-level price: made in small batches with high-quality ingredients - About $6.00 for 1 qt - 1 qt is minimum purchase - Suggested subscription: 1 qt per person per week (about ½ cup eaten each day)
Menu Base: - Cream + Milk (regular) - Skim Milk (low fat) - Coconut Cream + Coconut Milk (non-dairy) + Add-Ins: - Fruits - Nuts - Nut Butters - Spices - Honey - Agave Nectar - Molasses - Vanilla - Malt Powder - Cocoa Powder (can make several add-in categories like “sweeteners”)

Strategy

Target Audience

Roles Parents who play sports and/or have children in sports Parents or their children who have gastrointestinal maladies Parents who do yoga and/or have health-oriented goals for their family
Demographics:
Age: Parents: 25 - 55 Children: 2 - 17
Gender: More women, but some men also Education: Mostly college educated Marital Status: Mostly married Income: Middle-to-Upper Location: Urban to suburban

User Personas

Kristen Roberts
Age: 36 Profession: Part-time yoga instructor Spouse: Bill (35) Kids: 1 daughter, Emma (4) Values: Health-focused In free time: When not instructing yoga, she is at home making soaps, candles, and many other natural products to ship through sales from her website Issues: Between working and husband traveling a lot, she doesn’t have much time for cooking and has to order a lot of the family’s groceries online Possible Solution: Add healthy yogurt (protein, vitamins, probiotics) to her family’s diet, be able to get it through online orders and have it delivered
David Valdes
Age: 42 Profession: Construction Superintendent Spouse: Maria (40) Kids: 2 sons, Michael (6), William (3) Values: Family-oriented In free time: He spends lots of time at the beach with his family; he does nearly everything outside of work with his family Issues: David has gastrointestinal discomfort Possible Solution: When taken daily, probiotics in Microgurt may help in alleviating David’s gastrointestinal discomfort

User Needs

Website needs to enable the user to: - Find out if the supplier delivers to their area - Order product online - Discern product description and custom ingredient ordering - Set up subscription orders and scheduling - Estimate prices - See order history - Contact someone with questions or issues about an order

Client Needs

Website needs to enable the client to: - Sell product online that will be delivered - Provide a system for order customization - Provide reliable communication and customer support - Communicate quality of the ingredients - Communicate health benefits of probiotics

Outline of Scope

Content Requirements

Food Menu - show a list of pre-planned yogurt products as well as ingredients to choose from for custom yogurts - display pricing Images of product Text indicating product is for South Bay area only Contact section - list company phone number and email address; it may be necessary to have multiple numbers and emails for: - ordering - customer inquiries / support About section - describe the company and the quality of the ingredients Educational section - explain probiotics and best methods of consuming Testimonials FAQ section - where do you deliver? - what about dietary restrictions (allergens, dairy, etc.) - minimum order? - additional charge for delivery? - minimum time need for an order? - pricing structure? - contact?

Functionality Requirements

User enters delivery address and finds out if she is close enough to receive deliveries Form to fill out and submit for orders; add items to shopping cart Form to fill out for making changes / cancellation if necessary Purchase as a guest or create a user account Log in In addition to accessing order history, logging in also allows user to set up a subscription for regular intervals of delivery (probiotics are best consumed daily for beneficial results) - time and date of delivery Place order - submit order form - enter payment information - get order confirmation (on website and receive email)

The wireframes show the website navigation menu options changing depending on which page the user is on. I later changed this as can be seen in the high-fidelity prototypes. I found it made more sense to keep the menu choices always the same for consistency, and simply highlight the name of the page you’re currently on. Wireframes were created in Balsamiq.

Mobile Homepage Wireframe

Mood Board

The dark purples were used for buttons, but other than that, most colors were much lighter. As the website is selling a yogurt product, I wanted to go with soft colors that almost look like they could be considered “creamy”. The foods here depict high-quality ingredients. When ordering food, it’s important to have photos of the food you’re ordering. An addition I might make to this website if it were launched would be to have info pages of each ingredient. These pages could have information like country of origin and a photo. Mood Board was created in Sketch.
